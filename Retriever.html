Program Overview

The WebLogic Innovation Seminar (WInS) program was created out of a need to protect and expand the existing install base of WebLogic.  WebLogic is a key component of the overall revenue for FMW. The WInS program consists of several modules that correspond to key WebLogic features.  The modules contain a presentation as well as a video demonstration of each feature.  By providing video demos we can guarantee consistent results when giving customer demos.  However, SCs should seek to give live demos when/where possible

Goals and Objectives

The overarching goal of WInS is to protect and expand the existing WebLogic install base. This can be achieved by proactively reaching out to customers and educating them on WebLogic’s current and new features.  Many of our customers are still using BEA WebLogic and have not been shown the new features of Oracle WebLogic Server in quite some time.  JBoss is actively pursuing WebLogic accounts and is in contact with our customers on a frequent basis. As such, customers are up-to-date on JBoss features but not as much with WebLogic.

We need to be proactive with our customer outreach, and WInS provides the content and structure that you can use to defend your accounts!!

Areas of Focus

The modules of WInS are grouped into the categories below, and each module contains a presentaiton and a video demonstration:

Getting Started

How do I start the conversation about WInS with the customer?

  • Some reps have successfully offered to do a "WebLogic Health Check”, contacting the customer to see how they are doing with WebLogic. Most customers have at least one complaint or question, and are usually happy to hear from Oracle.
  • Some SCs position it as a PM roadshow to increase customer interest, and PM is happy to support critical customers. In the BEA days, the WebLogic roadshow was very well received by customers.
  • Many customers have unfortunately not heard from Oracle since the BEA acquisition. This likely means that they are using an old version of WebLogic. Version 8.1 was a great platform and we still have many customers running production apps on it today, however these customers are mostly not aware of the significant enhancements we have made to the platform, and many of them upgrade and buy more licenses when they become aware.
  • We have many customers still using iAS and we want them to migrate to WebLogic, and ideally to upgrade to WebLogic Suite. These customers need Oracle to educate them on WebLogic so they can understand and appreciate the features it offers above and beyond what OC4J/iAS can do.

How do I select modules?

  • Each module is designed to address a specific feature of WebLogic with a focus on the differentiated features in the product. Each module contains a presentation and a video demonstration. Videos were chosen as an approach to ensure high quality and consistent delivery of the content. Modules are designed to be delivered in a 1-hour time slot with approximately 45 minutes of presentation and 10-15 minutes of video demonstration. In order to design a session for a customer, an SC should select the modules that are most relevant to the individual customer. For example, if the customer is not using JMS and there is no opportunity to have them adopt WebLogic JMS, the JMS modules could be excluded.
  • Ideally, your team should know what your customers are using WebLogic for and should be able to select modules that would be interesting to them. However, this may not be the case for all accounts.
  • We would like customers to have exposure to multiple aspects of WebLogic. This being the case, it is good to encourage customers to select modules (or for you to propose modules) from multiple functional areas listed below.
  • We have several customers that have purchased WebLogic Suite but are not using all the features or products it comes with, such as Coherence. If you have a customer who falls into this category, it is helpful to choose modules that cover the products they have purchased, but are not actively using. When customers adopt our advanced and ‘sticky’ features, they are typically more satisfied with their investment, often buy more, and are less likely to move off of Oracle onto a competitor’s platform.
  • A solid approach is to provide the list of modules that we offer and give the customer the option of "ordering from the menu". This will ensure that the modules that you deliver are interesting to the customer.

How many modules should I select?

  • The number of modules depends on the amount of time that the customer is willing to commit to the workshop.
  • Typically, we find that one module per 45-60 minutes is a good goal, however if there are not many questions, some modules can be covered in 30 minutes.

How do I deliver each module?

  • First, deliver the presentation. The presentations are designed to be around 3 minutes each.
  • If you are capable and prepared to do so, deliver a live demo with VirtualBox (planned for FY13 - preferred). Otherwise, show the video and talk the customer through it.
  • Be sure to leave time for Q&A at the end. During the Q&A, challenge the customer to think

Structuring and Delivering the Workshop

Depending on the modules selected, you can work with the customer to group them for interested parties such that we make the most of their time. Sometimes the Develoeprs are not interested in Operations and vice versa.

Here are some examples that the field has used that are successful:

Single day events:

  • First half of hte day (3-4 hours): Developer Focused
  • Second half of hte day (3-4 hours): Operations Focused

Multi-day events:

  • Day 1: Developer Focused
  • Day 2: Operations Focused

Lunch and Learns

  • Two modules per day 11:30-1:30 or 12:00-2:00 for a week

What customers should I target?

Excellent customer targets for this program include, but are not limited to:
  • Those with a recently renewed ULA - to drive adoption of WebLogic for new projects.
  • Those with a ULA coming up for renewal - to remind them that WebLogic is the #1 App Server with the best features and lowest TCO.
  • Those where WebLogic is being considered – this is an active opportunity to educate them about the product.

Ideally, we want to talk to each and every WebLogic customer. There is no account that is too small or too large!
